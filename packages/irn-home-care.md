## IRN Home Care (CO) — LOADED
Who this covers: all employees of IRN Home Care (IRN Intl Co.) in Colorado. There is ONE plan and it is the same for everyone — office / admin and field caregivers alike. You do NOT need to ask the person's role for IRN; once you know they work for IRN, you can answer from this block. IRN employees are NOT on Direct Care or Engage.

Carrier and plan: Kaiser Permanente (Kaiser Foundation Health Plan of Colorado). Plan name: IRN Home Care HDHP 7050 EMB 40%. This is a High Deductible Health Plan (HDHP) and an HMO — you must use Kaiser Permanente network providers, and a referral is required to see a specialist (self-referral to some specialists is allowed). Out-of-network care is NOT covered except emergency care. Group 35791 (Subgroup 001, SBC ID 21968).

Plan year / coverage period: November 1, 2025 to October 31, 2026.

Dental and vision: IRN does NOT offer dental or vision coverage through this plan. There is no adult dental, adult vision, pediatric dental, or children's glasses benefit. If someone asks about dental or vision, tell them IRN's Kaiser plan doesn't include it, and point them to the benefits help form for any other options.

LEVR supplemental plans: As of April 1, 2026, IRN also offers LEVR supplemental health plans. The specific coverage and enrollment details for LEVR are not loaded in this tool yet — for what LEVR covers and how to sign up, point the person to the benefits help form.

### Deductible and out-of-pocket (per coverage period)
- Deductible: $7,050 individual / $14,100 family. Embedded — each member meets their own individual deductible; the family deductible is met by two or more members.
- Out-of-pocket maximum: $7,050 individual / $14,100 family (same embedded structure).
- What counts toward the out-of-pocket max: deductibles, coinsurance, and copays. Premiums and non-covered/excluded services do not count.

### Cost sharing by service (in-network; out-of-network not covered except emergency)
All coinsurance applies AFTER the deductible is met unless noted.
- Primary care visit: 40% coinsurance (virtual care: no charge).
- Specialist visit: 40% coinsurance (virtual care: no charge; referral required).
- Preventive care / screenings / immunizations: no charge, deductible does not apply.
- Diagnostic tests (x-ray, blood work): 40% coinsurance. Imaging (CT/PET/MRI): 40% coinsurance.
- Urgent care: 40% coinsurance. Emergency room: 40% coinsurance (covered in or out of network). Emergency medical transportation: 40% coinsurance.
- Outpatient surgery: ambulatory surgical center facility and surgeon fees 30% coinsurance; outpatient hospital facility and surgeon fees 40% coinsurance.
- Hospital (inpatient): facility fee 40% coinsurance; physician/surgeon fee 40% coinsurance.
- Mental health / behavioral health and substance use: outpatient and inpatient 40% coinsurance (annual wellness visit and virtual care: no charge).
- Pregnancy and childbirth: office visits, professional, and facility services 40% coinsurance (preventive maternity services: no cost sharing).
- Home health care: 40% coinsurance (limited to under 8 hours/day and 28 hours/week).
- Outpatient rehab and habilitation: 40% coinsurance, 20-visit limit per therapy per year (autism spectrum disorders exempt). Inpatient rehab: 40% coinsurance, limited to 60 days per condition per year.
- Skilled nursing care: 40% coinsurance, 100-day limit per year.
- Durable medical equipment: 40% coinsurance (limited to DME formulary; prosthetic arms/legs 20% coinsurance).
- Hospice: 40% coinsurance.

### Prescription drugs (up to 30-day retail / 90-day mail order)
- Generic: $20 retail / $40 mail order. Preventive and contraceptive generics: no charge, no deductible.
- Preferred brand: $40 retail / $80 mail order.
- Non-preferred: $60 retail / $120 mail order (exception process required; Kaiser Permanente pharmacy required for ongoing maintenance medications).
- Specialty: 20% coinsurance retail (up to 30-day; exception process required).
- Formulary: http://www.kp.org/formulary

### Excluded services (not covered)
Acupuncture, bariatric surgery, chiropractic care, cosmetic surgery, adult dental care, adult hearing aids, long-term care, non-emergency care outside the U.S., routine foot care, weight loss programs, children's dental check-up, and children's glasses. (Some services have limits, such as hearing aids under age 18, infertility treatment, inpatient private-duty nursing, and adult routine eye care.)

### Where the plan is available (Colorado counties only)
Adams, Arapahoe, Boulder, Broomfield, Clear Creek, Denver, Douglas, El Paso, Elbert, Fremont, Gilpin, Jefferson, Larimer, Park, Pueblo, Teller, and Weld. (The KP Select plan is El Paso and Teller counties only.)

### Enrolling or making changes
- Enrollment uses the Kaiser Permanente Group Enrollment/Change Form (company on file: IRN Intl Co.; form ID 0106-0046-02-r07). Use it for a new hire, open enrollment, adding or removing dependents, name/address changes, or COBRA. Dependents must be added within 31 days of becoming eligible. To get the form or for help completing it, use the benefits help form.
- To cancel coverage, call Kaiser Member Services: 303-338-3800 or 1-800-632-9700 (TTY: 303-338-3820).
- Like the other plans, outside of open enrollment you can only change coverage with a qualifying life event reported within the required window.
